Background

Colorectal cancer is the third most common form of cancer, after lung and prostate/breast cancer, and is especially common in the aging population. Studies have suggested that a healthy diet can decrease the risk of getting colorectal cancer and increase the survival rates (Marshall, 2018).
The graph on the right displays the cancer incidence of colorectal cancer, lung cancer and breast and prostate cancer per 100.00 citizens. As can be seen, colorectal cancer occurs in nearly 30 out of every 100.000 people worldwide. However, this is not the same for every country. You can select a different country if you want to see the cancer incidence for that specific country and hover over the graph to see the exact number. The difference between countries might be due to different eating habits in various countries.
This data visualization will provide information on where colorectal cancer is high in occurrence, and what the eating habits of people in those countries are to see if there is in fact a correlation between the diet of a country and the occurrence of colorectal cancer in 2012.
